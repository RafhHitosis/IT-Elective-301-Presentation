# Risks and Opportunities of E-commerce

## Presentation Content for Exam Preparation

### 1. Context: The Shift to Online

**Moving Online**

- Shopping has changed from physical shops to buying on phones and computers.
- Trillions of dollars are spent online every year.
- Most people shop using their mobile phones.
- It is not just about buying; it is about the experience.
- _Did you know?_ Even when the economy is bad, online sales keep growing. It is the future of business.

**Old vs. New (Comparison)**

- **Physical Store**:
  - Can only sell to locals.
  - High Rent Costs.
  - Closes at night.
  - Hard to count stock.
- **Online Store**:
  - Sell to the whole world.
  - Lower Costs.
  - Open 24/7.
  - Automatic tracking.

---

### 2. Opportunities (The Good Things)

**1. Sell to More People (Market Reach)**

- You are not limited by your city. A small shop can sell to customers in other countries easily.
- Find new customers far away.
- Sell special items to people who really want them.
- Exporting goods is much easier now.
- _Example (Gymshark)_: Started in a garage, used the internet to sell globally, now a huge brand.

**2. Save Money (Cost Reduction)**

- Running an online store is cheaper. No fancy building or lots of staff needed.
- **No Rent**: You don't pay for a physical shop.
- **Smart Stock**: Order items only when you sell them.
- **Less Staff**: Computers handle the payments.
- _Example (Dropshipping)_: Selling products you don't touch. Factory sends directly to customer. Zero stock held.

**3. Know Your Customer (Data Analytics)**

- Online, you can see exactly what people like. This helps you sell more effectively.
- See what they look at.
- Know when to order more stock.
- Send them offers they actually like.
- _Example (Netflix)_: Recommends movies based on what you like; online stores do the same for products.

**4. Open 24/7 (Availability)**

- Your store never sleeps. Make money while you sleep.
- Sell to people in different time zones.
- The store always looks perfect.
- Customers can buy late at night.
- _Example (Smart Chatbots)_: AI "robots" answer customer questions instantly at any time.

**5. Better Interaction (Engagement)**

- Websites can show videos, reviews, and interactive guides.
- Video demos show how products work.
- Customers can read reviews from others.
- Building a fan community is easier.
- _Example (Virtual Makeup)_: Apps that let you "try on" lipstick using your phone camera.

**6. Easy to Grow (Scalability)**

- If your business gets popular suddenly, it is easy to handle.
- Handle big sales days like Black Friday.
- Add thousands of new products instantly.
- Change your website look in one click.
- _Example (Black Friday)_: Online platforms handle millions of shoppers at once; physical shops would be too full.

**7. Personal Experience (Personalization)**

- Every customer is special. The website changes to match them.
- Special prices for loyal customers.
- Showing products based on history.
- Emails that address you by name.
- _Example (Nike By You)_: Designing your own sneakers makes the product feel unique to you.

---

### 3. Risks (The Dangers)

**1. Safety & Hackers (Cybersecurity)**

- Criminals are online too. Stolen data can destroy a business.
- **Ransomware**: Locking your computer for money.
- **Phishing**: Tricking you into giving passwords.
- **DDoS**: Attacking a site to shut it down.
- _Example (MGM Hotel Attack)_: Hackers shut down systems, costing millions.

**2. Too Much Competition**

- Easy to start means everyone is doing it.
- Competitors are just one click away.
- It costs more to show ads now.
- Big companies can sell things cheaper.
- _Example (Shein & Temu)_: Huge companies selling very cheap, making it hard for small businesses to compete.

**3. Shipping Problems (Logistics)**

- Sending packages is hard and expensive.
- Shipping costs are going up.
- Customers return clothes that don't fit.
- Packages can get lost or delayed.
- _Example (Global Delays)_: Ships stuck at ports can delay products for holidays, causing revenue loss.

**4. Reliance on Tech (Tech Dependence)**

- If the internet or servers break, your store is closed.
- If the server breaks, you lose sales.
- If Google changes rules, you might disappear.
- If your account is banned, business stops.
- _Example (System Crash)_: If a major provider like Amazon AWS crashes, thousands of sites go down.

**5. Laws & Rules (Regulatory)**

- Selling globally means following many different laws.
- Privacy laws about customer data.
- Different taxes in different countries.
- Accessibility for disabled people.
- _Example (Privacy Fines)_: Companies fined for not protecting user data properly.

**6. Trust Issues**

- Customers can't touch the product, so trust is key.
- Bad reviews can hurt sales.
- People are scared of scams.
- Worry about credit card safety.
- _Example (Reviews)_: A drop from 4 stars to 3 stars can stop sales.

---

### 4. Participants (Who is Involved?)

**1. B2C (Business to Consumer)**

- A business sells to a person (You).
- Buying a shirt, book, or phone.
- Fast, easy, emotional purchases.
- _Example_: Buying a toy from Amazon.

**2. B2B (Business to Business)**

- Companies selling to each other.
- Buying bulk supplies or software.
- Bigger contracts, takes longer.
- Logic-based decisions.
- _Example_: A factory selling 5000 t-shirts to a shop.

**3. C2C (Consumer to Consumer)**

- Regular people selling to other regular people.
- Selling used items or handmade crafts.
- Trust is key.
- _Example_: Selling an old bike on eBay or Vinted.

**4. C2B (Consumer to Business)**

- A person provides value to a business.
- Freelancers, influencers, surveys.
- _Example_: A photographer selling photos to an agency.

**5. Government & Administration**

- **B2G (Business to Gov)**: Construction firms, software for cities.
- **G2B (Gov to Business)**: Auctions, permits, data services.
- **G2C (Gov to Consumer)**: Parking tickets, passports.
- **C2G (Consumer to Gov)**: Taxes, voting, paying tuition.

---

### 5. The Future (What is Next?)

**1. AI Shopping Assistants**

- Talking to computers instead of searching.
- "Find me a blue suit for a summer wedding under $200" -> AI finds it.
- _Example_: Google Gemini understanding complex questions.

**2. Shopping in 3D (AR & Metaverse)**

- The store comes to your living room.
- VR glasses to walk around a virtual shop.
- _Example_: Seeing a life-size sofa in your room before buying.

**3. Green Shopping (Sustainability)**

- McLeaner shipping and less waste.
- Electric delivery vans, less plastic.
- _Example_: Reselling old clothes to reduce waste.
